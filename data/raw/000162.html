Toy Story 3: Movie Review

By
neoavatara
– June 18, 2010
\r\n
\r\n\t\t

\n

\n

It is pretty easy nowadays to write a review of a Pixar movie. \xc2\xa0Simply write, ‘Great!’, and end it there…and you would pretty much hit the nail on the head.

\n

The original Toy Story movies were the coming out party for Pixar 15 years ago. \xc2\xa0They introduced America to a new kind of animation…one that once again drew adults and children. \xc2\xa0And this movie, as much as any, taps into that.

\n

Andy, the toy’s owner, is all grown up and off to college. \xc2\xa0So the inevitable question: \xc2\xa0what do you do with all those childhood memories? \xc2\xa0Well, most of us throw them away, or give them away to charity. \xc2\xa0It is part of moving into adulthood (except for me, who keeps on buying toys for myself…).

\n

They accidentally make their way to a day care center, where they quickly realize…they are no longer really wanted. Their Andy is gone.

\n

A host of new toys make appearances, including Ken (of Barbie fame) and Big Baby…a scary looking doll if there was ever one (a spawn of Chucky?). \xc2\xa0Any parent who gives a Big Baby doll to their child should be separated from all children for eternity.

\n

Now, this movie is in many ways dark. \xc2\xa0It is not like the first two movies, which were largely fun jaunts through playland. \xc2\xa0There are serious issues at hand here, including how change affects life. There is a scene a dumpyard that I don’t think my kid understood…but it shows you the wasteland that awaits all toys. \xc2\xa0It was, in its own way, scary and moving.

\n

Ironically, the Toy Story movies are the Pixar movies I least watch. \xc2\xa0Maybe they are too oriented for kids, I don’t know. \xc2\xa0But this film is one that I really think I want to go back and see.

\n

This movie is gorgeous in 3-D., especially IMAX, and that is certainly preferable to a regular theater if you have the opportunity. \xc2\xa0Animation is ideal for 3D, and this may be the best of the animated 3D films out there.

\n

Another Pixar movie classic….but is there any other kind?

\n
