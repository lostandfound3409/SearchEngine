\nAlice Kingsley:\nI\'m sorry, Hamish. I can\'t marry you. You\'re not the right man for me. And there\'s that trouble with your digestion.

\n[Alice walks over to her sister]\n

\nAlice Kingsley:\nI love you, Margaret, but this is my life. I\'ll decide what to do with it.

\n[Alice turns to Lowell, meeting his sour look with a stern glare]\n

\nAlice Kingsley:\nYou\'re lucky to have my sister for your wife, Lowell, and be good to her. I\'ll be watching very closely.

\n[Alice goes up to Aunt Imogene and takes her hands]\n

\nAlice Kingsley:\nThere is no prince, Aunt Imogene. You need to talk to someone about these delusions.

\n[Alice walks past Lady Ascot, looking sternly at her]\n

\nAlice Kingsley:\nI happen to love rabbits, especially white ones.

\n[Alice goes up to her mother]\n

\nAlice Kingsley:\nDon\'t worry, Mother. I\'ll find something useful to do with my life.

\n[Alice turns to the Chattaway sisters]\n

\nAlice Kingsley:\nYou two remind me of some funny boys I met in a dream.

\nLord Ascot:\nYou\'ve left me out.

\nAlice Kingsley:\nNo, I haven\'t, sir. You and I have business to discuss.

\nLord Ascot:\nShall we speak in the study?

\n[Alice smiles and starts to head to the house, when suddenly she turns back to the crowd]\n

\nAlice Kingsley:\nOh. And one more thing.

\n[Alice lifts the hem of her dress to her knees and does the Futterwacken]\n

\nCheshire Cat:\n[suddenly appearing]\n

\n[notices the marks on Alice\'s arm]\n

\nCheshire Cat:\nIt looks like you ran afoul of something with wicked claws.

\nAlice Kingsley:\n[to herself]\nI\'ve never stopped dreaming.

\nCheshire Cat:\nWhat did that to you?

\nAlice Kingsley:\nBander who... the bander...

\nCheshire Cat:\nThe Bandersnatch?

\n[suddenly appears behind Alice, she turns to face him]\n

\nCheshire Cat:\nWell, I\'d better have a look.

\nAlice Kingsley:\nWhat are you doing?

\nCheshire Cat:\nIt needs to be purified by someone with evaporting skills, or it will fester and putrefy.

\nAlice Kingsley:\nI\'d rather you didn\'t. I\'ll be fine as soon as I wake up.

\nCheshire Cat:\nAt least let me bind it for you?

\n[Alice holds out her arm and he wraps the wound with a piece of cloth]\n

\nCheshire Cat:\nWhat do you call yourself?

\nAlice Kingsley:\nAlice.

\nCheshire Cat:\n*The* Alice?

\nAlice Kingsley:\nThere\'s been some debate about that.

\nCheshire Cat:\nI never get involved in politics.

\n[disappears]\n
