Joss Whedon on Avengers influences, defends Spider-Man 3

\nJoss Whedon discusses his many influences on Avengers: Age of Ultron, grounding the villain's powers, and defends Spider-Man 3...

Right: Oscars and awards done with for another year. It\'s nearly blockbuster season now. And few are\xc2\xa0bigger or enjoying more anticipation than Joss Whedon\'s Avengers: Age Of Ultron, the sequel to the third highest grossing film of all time.

\n

For his encore, Joss Whedon appears not to be resting on his laurels. Indeed, in his latest interview with Empire, The Avengers director certainly made it clear his ambitions were fairly high, even purely in terms of influence.

\n

"I know I reference Godfather Part II a lot" he said. "\'Don\xe2\x80\x99t reference the greatest movie ever made because that\xe2\x80\x99s Icarus, you moron!\xe2\x80\x99 But at the same time, it is a huge touchstone for me, because you get everything you got from the first Godfather movie in a very different movie, in a movie that structurally couldn\xe2\x80\x99t be more different, and thematically and in intent and in mood, and yet nobody ever goes \xe2\x80\x98it wasn\xe2\x80\x99t Godfather-y enough\'. I need to give people an exciting ride about heroic people, and that\xe2\x80\x99s certainly part of why I signed on, but at the same time, a richer, deeper, darker movie is not a bad thing".

\n

In that vein, Whedon also revealed that he has a new kind of villain that he is grounding to be the megalomaniacal foil to his superheroes.

\n

"The powers in the comic books \xe2\x80\x93 they\xe2\x80\x99re always like \'and then I can reverse the polarity of your ions\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x93 well, we have to ground things a lot more. With Ultron, we have to make him slightly less omnipotent, because he\xe2\x80\x99d win. Bottom line. Also, having weaknesses and needs, and foibles, and alliances, and actually caring what people think of him, all these things, are what make him a character and not just a tidal wave. A movie about a tidal wave can be great, but it\xe2\x80\x99s different than a conflict between one side and the other...[so] the powers he has are slightly different \xe2\x80\x93 he can control certain things, he\xe2\x80\x99s not just firing repulsors".

\n

And then Whedon sprung to the defence of Sam Raimi\'s maligned (including by Raimi himself!)\xc2\xa0Spider-Man 3.

\n

When discussing Quicksilver and Scarlet Witch, he revealed that "they\xe2\x80\x99re already good to go by the time we\xe2\x80\x99re up and running. You don\xe2\x80\x99t want to fall into Spider-Man 3 territory \xe2\x80\x93 and I say that as a guy who actually thinks pretty well of that movie; there\xe2\x80\x99s some great stuff in that movie \xe2\x80\x93 but there comes a point where you\xe2\x80\x99re overloaded with front-story, back-story, origin story, and it becomes very hard to juggle".

\n

The full Empire interview is here.

\n

Avengers: Age Of Ultron lands in UK cinemas at the end of April.
