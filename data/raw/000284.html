JoeTyler

I loved and love that album

\n

\n

A perfect sequel to the spiritual/serious Ray of Light. An earthy album mixing both organic and electronic music, dance jams and also guitar-oriented ballads. And I love the puns against the whole "female singer/songwriter" genre of the 96-2001 era, lol, starting with the front cover. She was obvioulsy mocking that movement and, at the same time, trying to get some $ out of it as well...

\n

\n

The only weak song for me was Nobody\'s Perfect, too slow... but the rest of the album was stellar. And the title-track was THE jam of 2000... I seriously think that Gone is her most underrated ballad, ever...

\n

\n

American Life was a similar album but bleaker and with lesser material, and I don\'t know why, but the electroclash thing sounded cheap. That\'s why I don\'t care for that album. It\'s Madonna following the blueprint of a previous album, something that she never did before, not even with Like a Virgin-True Blue, which were reasonably different albums if you think about it...

\n

\n

Anyway, I can\'t believe Music is already 11 years old ...omg time flies! which is both and

Tortilla

I remember being in love with this album when it came out. Haven\'t listened to it as a whole in a long time.

\n

\n

It\'s all over the place, but its length works in favor of the disjointed feeling. Naming the album Music -- there\'s nothing really meaningful behind it really, right? The album feels like a collection of songs without a real concept. Just some tunes that she (and her collaborators) put together.

\n

\n

The contrast of her image with the sound was pretty interesting. It was the most electronic (read: synthetic) she had gone up til that junction of her career, but dressed in Americana (read: organic) style clothing.

\n

\n

Can\'t say it\'s in the top five, but it does have one of my favorite ballads -- "Paradise (Not for Me)".
