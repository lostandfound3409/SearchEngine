Lolita

Vladimir Nabokov

\n\n \n
←
Plot Overview
→

Context

Character List

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n \n

In the novel’s foreword, the fictional John Ray, Jr.,\nPh.D., explains the strange story that will follow. According to\nRay, he received the manuscript, entitled Lolita, or the\nConfession of a White Widowed Male, from the author’s lawyer.\nThe author himself, known by the pseudonym of Humbert Humbert (or\nH. H.), died in jail of coronary thrombosis while awaiting a trial.\nRay asserts that while the author’s actions are despicable, his\nwriting remains beautiful and persuasive. He also indicates that\nthe novel will become a favorite in psychiatric circles as well\nas encourage parents to raise better children in a better world.

\n
\n

In the manuscript, Humbert relates his peaceful upbringing\non the Riviera, where he encounters his first love, the twelve-year-old Annabel\nLeigh. Annabel and the thirteen-year-old Humbert never consummate\ntheir love, and Annabel’s death from typhus four months later haunts\nHumbert. Although Humbert goes on to a career as a teacher of English\nliterature, he spends time in a mental institution and works a succession\nof odd jobs. Despite his marriage to an adult woman, which eventually\nfails, Humbert remains obsessed with sexually desirable and sexually\naware young girls. These nymphets, as he calls them, remind him\nof Annabel, though he fails to find another like her. Eventually,\nHumbert comes to the United States and takes a room in the house\nof widow Charlotte Haze in a sleepy, suburban New England town.\nHe becomes instantly infatuated with her twelve-year-old daughter\nDolores, also known as Lolita. Humbert follows Lolita’s moves constantly,\noccasionally flirts with her, and confides his pedophiliac longings\nto a journal. Meanwhile, Charlotte Haze, whom Humbert loathes, has fallen\nin love with him. When Charlotte sends Lolita off to summer camp,\nHumbert marries Charlotte in order to stay near his true love. Humbert\nwants to be alone with Lolita and even toys with the idea of killing\nCharlotte, but he can’t go through with it. However, Charlotte finds\nhis diary and, after learning that he hates her but loves her daughter,\nconfronts him. Humbert denies everything, but Charlotte tells him\nshe is leaving him and storms out of the house. At that moment,\na car hits her and she dies instantly.

\n

Humbert goes to the summer camp and picks up Lolita. Only when\nthey arrive at a motel does he tell her that Charlotte has died. In\nhis account of events, Humbert claims that Lolita seduces him, rather\nthan the other way around. The two drive across the country for\nnearly a year, during which time Humbert becomes increasingly obsessed\nwith Lolita and she learns to manipulate him. When she engages in\ntantrums or refuses his advances, Humbert threatens to put her in\nan orphanage. At the same time, a strange man seems to take an interest\nin Humbert and Lolita and appears to be following them in their\ntravels.

\n

Humbert eventually gets a job at Beardsley College somewhere in\nthe Northeast, and Lolita enrolls in school. Her wish to socialize with\nboys her own age causes a strain in their relationship, and Humbert\nbecomes more restrictive in his rules. Nonetheless, he allows her\nto appear in a school play. Lolita begins to behave secretively\naround Humbert, and he accuses her of being unfaithful and takes\nher away on another road trip. On the road, Humbert suspects that\nthey are being followed. Lolita doesn’t notice anything, and Humbert\naccuses her of conspiring with their stalker.

\n

Lolita becomes ill, and Humbert must take her to the hospital. However,\nwhen Humbert returns to get her, the nurses tell him that her uncle\nhas already picked her up. Humbert flies into a rage, but then he\ncalms himself and leaves the hospital, heartbroken and angry.

\n

For the next two years, Humbert searches for Lolita, unearthing clues\nabout her kidnapper in order to exact his revenge. He halfheartedly\ntakes up with a woman named Rita, but then he receives a note from\nLolita, now married and pregnant, asking for money. Assuming that\nLolita has married the man who had followed them on their travels,\nHumbert becomes determined to kill him. He finds Lolita, poor and\npregnant at seventeen. Humbert realizes that Lolita’s husband is\nnot the man who kidnapped her from the hospital. When pressed, Lolita\nadmits that Clare Quilty, a playwright whose presence has been felt\nfrom the beginning of the book, had taken her from the hospital.\nLolita loved Quilty, but he kicked her out when she refused to participate\nin a child pornography orgy. Still devoted to Lolita, Humbert begs\nher to return to him. Lolita gently refuses. Humbert gives her 4,000\ndollars and then departs. He tracks down Quilty at his house and\nshoots him multiple times, killing him. Humbert is arrested and\nput in jail, where he continues to write his memoir, stipulating\nthat it can only be published upon Lolita’s death. After Lolita\ndies in childbirth, Humbert dies of heart failure, and the manuscript\nis sent to John Ray, Jr., Ph.D.

\n\n

\n \n

Lolita is a child in the early stages of puberty. Humbert, being attracted to such girls, is technically a hebephile, not a pedophile.
