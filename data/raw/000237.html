What Spider-Man coming to Marvel does and doesn\xe2\x80\x99t mean


By JEFF PETERSON
DESERET NEWS

It\xe2\x80\x99s finally happened: Spider-Man will join the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

In an announcement that has brought smiles to the faces of comic geeks everywhere, Sony and Marvel jointly revealed via a press release that ol\xe2\x80\x99 web-head will take his place alongside Iron Man, the Hulk and the rest of the Avengers on the big screen, and soon \xe2\x80\x94 most likely in the upcoming \xe2\x80\x9cCaptain America: Civil War.\xe2\x80\x9d

The deal was announced Monday night after months of speculation about Spidey\xe2\x80\x99s possible movie future in the wake of the less-than-stellar box office for \xe2\x80\x9cThe Amazing Spider-Man 2.\xe2\x80\x9d

According to the press release, \xe2\x80\x9cthe new Spider-Man will first appear in a Marvel film from Marvel\xe2\x80\x99s Cinematic Universe. Sony Pictures will thereafter release the next installment of its $4 billion Spider-Man franchise, on July 28, 2017.\xe2\x80\x9d

This development is a huge win for both Marvel and Sony \xe2\x80\x94 not to mention the fans clamoring for a more integrated superhero universe \xe2\x80\x94 but there\xe2\x80\x99s potential for some confusion about what this actually means going forward, so let\xe2\x80\x99s break it down.

Out with the old Spider-Man, in with the new

One question that\xe2\x80\x99s popped up is whether or not \xe2\x80\x9cThe Amazing Spider-Man\xe2\x80\x9d\xe2\x80\x99s Andrew Garfield will reprise his role as Peter Parker. Although not directly addressed by the press release, it makes numerous references to a \xe2\x80\x9cnew Spider-Man.\xe2\x80\x9d

According to an unnamed source for the Daily Beast, the actor met to discuss the possibility of returning before all agreed that it would not make sense.

News outlets ranging from the Wall Street Journal to The Wrap have backed that up saying Garfield is out and Marvel is now on the hunt for a younger actor to play a high school-aged Spider-Man, with Variety naming \xe2\x80\x9cPercy Jackson\xe2\x80\x9d star Logan Lerman and \xe2\x80\x9cThe Maze Runner\xe2\x80\x9d\xe2\x80\x99s Dylan O\xe2\x80\x99Brien as two candidates already being eyed by Marvel and Sony, although no actors have been approached.

Other names that have been tossed around, according to Dark Horizons, include Taron Egerton (\xe2\x80\x9cKingsman: The Secret Service\xe2\x80\x9d), Asa Butterfield (\xe2\x80\x9cEnder\xe2\x80\x99s Game\xe2\x80\x9d), Donald Glover (\xe2\x80\x9cCommunity\xe2\x80\x9d), Zac Efron (\xe2\x80\x9cHigh School Musical\xe2\x80\x9d) and current Best Actor nominee Eddie Redmayne (\xe2\x80\x9cThe Theory of Everything\xe2\x80\x9d). No official announcements have been made though. In fact, it\xe2\x80\x99s not even clear yet if this Spider-Man will still be Peter Parker and not, say, the Ultimate line of comics\xe2\x80\x99 Spider-Man, Miles Morales. So take that all with a grain of salt.

Marvel still does not own the film rights

Amidst all the hoopla surrounding the news of Spider-Man appearing in the MCU, there has been a lot of talk of him returning to Marvel.

Not that this should necessarily temper the excitement, but to be clear, Spider-Man\xe2\x80\x99s film rights \xe2\x80\x94 and therefore, creative control \xe2\x80\x94 still belong to Sony, as is made explicit in the press release: \xe2\x80\x9cSony Pictures will continue to finance, distribute, own and have final creative control of the Spider-Man films.\xe2\x80\x9d

In fact, according to The Hollywood Reporter, this deal did not even involve any money changing hands between the two rival studios; it was brokered on an entirely quid pro quo basis.

Taking a pessimistic view of things, this could ultimately turn out not to be as big an improvement as fans might hope since this is still the same Sony that pushed Sam Raimi to include Venom in \xe2\x80\x9cSpider-Man 3\xe2\x80\x9d and rebooted the franchise a mere five years later and whose interference, according to Garfield, may have sabotaged \xe2\x80\x9cThe Amazing Spider-Man 2.\xe2\x80\x9d

The key difference will be in the producers. The new Spider-Man film, says the press release, \xe2\x80\x9cwill be co-produced by Kevin Feige and his expert team at Marvel and Amy Pascal, who oversaw the franchise launch for the studio 13 years ago. Together, they will collaborate on a new creative direction for the web-slinger.\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cThe Amazing Spider-Man\xe2\x80\x9d\xe2\x80\x99s production team of Matt Tolmach and Avi Arad, meanwhile, will be demoted to executive producer status, meaning they will have no creative input.

Of possible concern to fans are more recent reports from sites like comicbook.com saying that Sony\xe2\x80\x99s other Spider-Man-related projects like \xe2\x80\x9cThe Sinister Six,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cVenom\xe2\x80\x9d and an as yet unnamed female-led superhero movie (possibly \xe2\x80\x9cBlack Cat\xe2\x80\x9d or \xe2\x80\x9cSpider-Woman\xe2\x80\x9d) are all still in development but that Feige will not be creatively involved with any of them.

Fingers crossed this does not include the rumored Aunt May prequel film that supposedly retooled the character as a swinging 1960s spy. I kid you not.

Presumably, whatever Spider-Man spinoffs are produced will be brought into line with the new direction and the larger MCU, but for many fans, Feige\xe2\x80\x99s involvement would do a lot to guarantee a certain level of quality.

This could be just the beginning of the studios\xe2\x80\x99 collaboration

In addition to Spider-Man appearing in Marvel films, negotiations are also reportedly under way to possibly have Marvel characters appear in future Sony Spider-Man movies.

This opens up interesting possibilities to see some classic Marvel team-ups on the big screen, such as, for instance, Spidey and his Hell\xe2\x80\x99s Kitchen neighbor, Daredevil, who is also in the process of being rebooted by Marvel with a Netflix series premiering April 10.

The two characters share many of the same villains, including Daredevil\xe2\x80\x99s main baddie, Kingpin, so it would make a lot of sense for them to join forces. Additionally, it would be a smart way for Marvel to bring Charlie Cox\xe2\x80\x99s Daredevil to the big screen without having to do a standalone Daredevil movie.

As one of Marvel\xe2\x80\x99s most beloved characters, Spider-Man has crossed paths with pretty much everyone, though, so the possibilities are endless.

So, what about the other non-MCU Marvel properties?

Now that Spider-Man has returned to the fold (sort of), fans are already wondering how long before Marvel strikes a similar deal with Fox to have the X-Men and Fantastic Four join the MCU.

The simple answer is probably \xe2\x80\x9cdon\xe2\x80\x99t hold your breath.\xe2\x80\x9d

Whereas \xe2\x80\x9cThe Amazing Spider-Man 2\xe2\x80\x9d\xe2\x80\x99s box-office numbers left Sony doubting the direction it had taken with the wall-crawler \xe2\x80\x94 it was the lowest-grossing of all five Spider-Man movies \xe2\x80\x94 Fox is currently riding high on the success of \xe2\x80\x9cX-Men: Days of Future Past.\xe2\x80\x9d With \xe2\x80\x9cFantastic Four\xe2\x80\x9d coming out later this year and the Ryan Reynolds-led \xe2\x80\x9cDeadpool\xe2\x80\x9d and Channing Tatum-led \xe2\x80\x9cGambit\xe2\x80\x9d in the pipeline, Fox already has its shared universe to play in.

After hearing the news of the Sony/Marvel deal, Hugh Jackman, who has talked openly in the past about wanting to see more collaboration between the studios so Wolverine could someday tussle onscreen with Iron man, told geek.com, \xe2\x80\x9cThere\xe2\x80\x99s so many forces at play there, man, beyond what anyone would want. The thing I\xe2\x80\x99ve always loved about the comic-book world is how the fun thing was how a writer of a comic book could just pull all these characters together and what became a Friday-night discussion of \xe2\x80\x98it would be cool to see Batman fight Wolverine\xe2\x80\x99 and bang, Monday morning they\xe2\x80\x99re working on it, you know. But that\xe2\x80\x99s an idealized world.\xe2\x80\x9dIf there\xe2\x80\x99s one thing this new deal proves, though, it\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cnever say never\xe2\x80\x9d when it comes to the Marvel Cinematic Universe.
